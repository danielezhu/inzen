**Farnam Jahanian** is an Iranian-born American computer scientist and
academic. He serves as the interim president of Carnegie Mellon University.

## Early life[edit]

Farnam Jahanian was born in Iran.[1] He earned a master's degree and a PhD in
computer science from the University of Texas at Austin.[2]

## Career[edit]

Jahanian began his career at the Thomas J. Watson Research Center.[2] In 1993,
he joined the department of computer science and engineering at the University
of Michigan, and was later promoted as the Edward S. Davidson collegiate
professor.[2] He was also the department chair from 2007 to 2011.[2] At the
same time, he was the co-founder and chairman of Arbor Networks from 2001 to
2010.[2]

Jahanian was the head of the National Science Foundation Directorate for
Computer and Information Science and Engineering from 2011 to 2014.[2] He was
the provost and chief academic officer of Carnegie Mellon University from 2015
to 2017.[2] Since July 1, 2017, he has served as its president, succeeding
Subra Suresh.[2][3]

Jahanian was named a "great immigrant" by the Carnegie Corporation of New York
in 2016.[4]

## Personal life[edit]

Jahanian has a wife, Tris, and three children.[2]
