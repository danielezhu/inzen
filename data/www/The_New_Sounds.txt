The New Sounds (PRLP 124) is the debut studio album of Miles Davis, released as a 1951 10-inch LP. It is his first album as leader, and his first complete album for Prestige Records. Davis had previously contributed three tracks to the various artists LP Modern Jazz Trumpets (PRLP 113), and performed as a sideman on Lee Konitz: The New Sounds (PRLP 116).
